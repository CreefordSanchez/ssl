Research and Writing: SSL

Article about explaining SSL function

What is SSL?

SSL stands for Secure Sockets Layer, and it is used to secure internet connections by encrypting the data exchanged between the client and the server. This encryption helps to prevent hackers from seeing, stealing, or altering the data during transmission, ensuring privacy and data integrity.

What is TLS?

TLS stands for Transport Layer Security. TLS provides the same functions as SSL but offers more improved security and efficiency compared to SSL. It's is used to encrypt data exchanged between a clients browser and a server, ensuring secure internet connections.

What is encryption and why do I care?

Encryption is a process of converting your data or information into a code using algorithms to transform it into an unreadable format. This prevents hackers from reading your personal information or data.

What does it mean to be secure?

Being secure means that measures are in place to protect against threats and vulnerabilities. In the context it means that your information wont be leaked, youre interacting with the legitimate website, and data exchanges wont be tampered with.

How can I tell that my webpage is secure?

A locked padlock icon in the address bar of your browser means the connection is secured with encryption. If it appears unlocked, your connection is not secure.

Do the prefixes HTTP or HTTPS matter when we're talking about secure websites?

A secure website URL should begin with "https" rather than "http" as it uses encryption, while an HTTP URL does not use encryption.

What is an SSL certificate authority?

SSL certificate authority (CA) is a trusted entity that issues and verifies SSL/TLS certificates, which are used to encrypt and secure communications between websites and users.
